# JEWELFLAME — Design Document

## Project Overview
A spiritual successor to **Gemfire** (SNES, 1992) — a turn-based grand strategy game with tactical grid combat.

**Core Hook:** "Fantasy Wars of the Roses" — strategic territory conquest meets tactical battles, distinguished by powerful limited-use wizard spells (the "Fifth Unit" system).

---

## Design Pillars

1. **Accessible Depth** — Easier to learn than Crusader Kings, deeper than Risk
2. **Meaningful Choices** — Every turn matters; resources are tight
3. **Tactical Puzzles** — Battles are small, discrete challenges
4. **Power Fantasy** — Wizards provide game-changing moments

---

## Game Structure

### Victory Conditions
- **Primary:** Capture all 30 territories of the realm
- **Secondary:** Defeat the Tyrant King (capturing his capital effectively ends the game)
- **Tactical Victory:**
  - Capture enemy base/flag
  - Eliminate all enemy units
  - Defensive: Hold for 5 turns

### Campaign Modes
- **Scenario Mode:** Multiple starting scenarios with different factions/years
- **Conquest Mode:** Single-faction unification challenge
- **Multiplayer:** Hotseat or networked competitive play

---

## Strategic Layer (Overworld)

### The Map
- **Setting:** Fantasy realm (England-shaped island vibe)
- **Territories:** 30 provinces, each with:
  - Castle/Fortress (defensive structure)
  - Agricultural rating (food production)
  - Economic rating (gold generation)
  - Ownership flag

### Turn Structure
Monthly turns cycling through seasons:
- **Spring:** Primary harvest (+Food)
- **Summer:** Standard operations
- **Fall:** Secondary harvest (reduced Food)
- **Winter:** Reduced movement, army starvation risk

### Territory Actions (Choose One Per Turn)
| Action | Function | Cost/Risk |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| **Develop** | Improve agriculture/castle defense | Consumes Gold, multi-turn |
| **Train** | Improve army quality/defense | Consumes Food + Gold |
| **Trade** | Exchange Gold ↔ Food | Market fluctuation |
| **Diplomacy** | Ally, Plunder, Sabotage, Defection | Failure damages relations |
| **Move** | Transfer units between adjacent territories | Vulnerable to interception |
| **Attack** | Invade adjacent enemy territory | Triggers tactical battle |

### Resource Economy
- **Gold:** Generated by territories, used for recruitment/development
- **Food:** Consumed monthly by armies; shortage causes desertion/battle penalties

**Army Maintenance:**
- Each unit consumes Food per turn
- Negative Food → morale penalties, attrition
- Winter exacerbates consumption

## Tactical Battle System

### Battlefield
- **Grid:** Square grid (not hex)
- **Size:** Variable (12×12 to 15×15 depending on terrain)
- **Terrain Types:**
  - Plains (standard)
  - Forests (defensive bonus, movement penalty)
  - Mountains (impassable to most, high defense)
  - Water/Rivers (impassable or bridge-only)
  - Castles (high defensive bonuses)

### Unit Roster
**Standard Units (4 slots per army):**
| Unit | Movement | Attack | Defense | Special |
|------|----------|--------|---------|---------|
| Cavalry | 3 | High | Medium | Charge bonus |
| Archers | 2 | Medium | Low | Ranged (2-3 range) |
| Heavy Knights | 2 | High | High | Balanced frontline |
| Light Infantry | 3 | Low | Low | Scout/cannon fodder |

**Fifth Unit System:**
- Dedicated slot for special units
- Swappable between battles
- Options: Wizards (limited spells), Mercenaries, Monsters

### Combat Mechanics
- **Initiative:** I-Go-You-Go (Player moves all, then AI)
- **Attacks:** Adjacent only (except Archers)
- **Damage:** (Attacker Attack + Terrain) - (Defender Defense + Terrain) ± variance
- **Morale:** Below 50% HP = penalties; Food shortage = penalties

## The Jewel System (Fifth Units)

Six wizards representing elemental powers. Each usable once per 3-month cooldown.

| Jewel | Wizard | Element | Power | Movement | Notes |
|-------|--------|---------|-------|----------|-------|
| Ruby | Fire Dragon | Fire | Extreme | 3 | Tyrant exclusive; game-ending |
| Emerald | Empyron | Fire Magic | Medium | 3 | Crystal fire attacks |
| Topaz | Zendor | Lightning | Very High | 3 | Best balance; speed + power |
| Sapphire | Pluvius | Meteors | Extreme | 2 | Highest damage, slowest |
| Aquamarine | Chylla | Ice | Medium | 3 | Freezing attacks |
| Amethyst | Scylla | Wind | Medium | 3 | Wind attacks |
| Pearl | Skulryk | Poison | Low | 3 | Poison clouds; weakest |

**Strategic Implications:**
- Wizards provide "alpha strike" to break stalemates
- Cooldown management is critical
- Zendor = optimal; Pluvius = high risk/reward

- **Tyrant's Faction:** Holds Fire Dragon; antagonist
- Notable: Contains a notoriously disloyal general (easy defection target)

### Character Stats
- **War:** Combat effectiveness (attack/defense bonuses)
- **Command:** Army leadership (morale, unit caps)
- **Politics:** Diplomatic effectiveness (defection, alliances)

## Diplomacy System

### Actions
- **Ally:** Non-aggression pact + potential military aid
- **Defection:** Recruit enemy generals (Politics stat matters)
- **Plunder:** Raid for resources (damages relations)
- **Sabotage:** Damage enemy development
- **Negotiate:** Peace treaties, territory exchanges

### AI Behavior
- Opportunistic: Allies when weak, breaks when strong
- Dogpile: Multiple AIs coordinate against powerful player
- Endgame: Predictably hostile when reduced to 2 factions

## Technical Architecture (Godot 4)

### Scene Structure
```
Game (Autoload)
├── GameState — Persistent data, save/load
├── EventBus — Cross-system communication
├── AudioManager — Music/SFX
└── SceneManager — Screen transitions

Screens
├── StrategicMap — Overworld layer
├── TacticalBattle — Combat layer
├── DiplomacyUI — Negotiations
└── FamilyManagement — Characters, units
```

### Data Models
- **Territory:** Owner, resources, garrison, development level
- **Unit:** Type, HP, stats, experience
- **Family:** Leader, generals, relations, jewels held
- **Wizard:** Type, cooldown remaining, uses remaining

### Save System
- JSON-based
- Territory state, unit positions, character stats
- Multiple save slots
- Auto-save before battles
